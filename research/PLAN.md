# Master Research Plan — Who Speaks the Commandment?

## The dispute being adjudicated
Tomer claims: the fire prohibition (Exodus 35:3, "לֹא תְבַעֲרוּ אֵשׁ") is the rare case where a commandment appears ONLY in Moses's mouth ("וַיֹּאמֶר מֹשֶׁה... אֵלֶּה הַדְּבָרִים אֲשֶׁר צִוָּה יְהוָה"), with no direct divine-speech ("A-frame") source for the specific content. He further claims that for laws like kashrut and Shabbat there IS a direct divine command first, then a duplicate where Moses repeats it to the people.

The manager (Opus) claims: the conduit is identical for most commandments — the majority reach us as Moses reporting "asher tziva Hashem," so the fire prohibition is not uniquely suspect.

This is a TEXTUAL question and the corpus will decide it.

## Three classification axes
1. **Speaker frame:**
   - A = direct divine speech. Narrator: "וַיְדַבֵּר יְהוָה אֶל מֹשֶׁה לֵּאמֹר" then content in God's voice. (Content inside a לֵאמֹר frame counts as A even though Moses relays it.)
   - B = Moses-attributed. Moses tells the people "אֵלֶּה הַדְּבָרִים אֲשֶׁר צִוָּה יְהוָה" with NO A-frame parallel for the same specific content.
   - C = narrative / initiative with no divine attribution.
2. **Permanence:** eternal/perpetual (חֻקַּת עוֹלָם, לְדֹרֹתֵיכֶם, בְּרִית עוֹלָם) vs one-time directive (e.g. wipe out Amalek, cross the Red Sea, build the Mishkan now).
3. **Decisive test:** for each commandment — does it have a direct A-frame source with the specific content, or only B?

## Methodological rulings (fixed)
- Exodus–Numbers is the test arena (narrator alternates between God's voice and Moses's voice). Deuteronomy is the whole-book Moses retelling (מִשְׁנֵה תּוֹרָה) and is analyzed SEPARATELY, not lumped into "Moses's mouth."
- "לֵאמֹר" content = A-frame (God told Moses to transmit).
- Quote every verse in Hebrew WITH nikud where possible, plus chapter:verse and a short modern-Hebrew gloss.

## Deliverable
A table: commandment | A-source (direct divine) | B-source (Moses-attributed) | permanence | classification — plus a closed list of "Moses-only" commandments and a verdict on kashrut and Shabbat.
